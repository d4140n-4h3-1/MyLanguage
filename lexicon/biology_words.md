### Extended Family

| English             | Singular        | Plural          |
|---------------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| ancestor            | *vorelkir*      | *vorelkirum*    |
| descendant          | *naxberkir*     | *naxberkirum*   |
| uncle               | *peokir*        | *peokirum*      |
| aunt                | *meorokir*      | *meorokirum*    |
| cousin / kinsman    | *makir*         | *makirum*       |
| male cousin         | *freomakir*     | *freomakirum*   |
| female cousin       | *seormakir*     | *seormakirum    |
| elder cousin        | *elmakir*       | *elmakirum*     |
| elder male cousin   | *elfreomakir*   | *elfreomakirum* |
| elder female cousin | *elseoromakir*  | *elseoromakir*  |
| nephew              | *sunamakir*     | *sunamakirum*   |
| niece               | *deoromakir*    | *deoromakirum*  |

Take notice of "-kir". It's inspired by "kin". If it's brothers and sisters in a organizational sense, it the "k" is replaced by "þ". 

If you look at "peokir", it shows kinship between one's parent and brother. The same goes for "meorokir", which is kinship between one's parent and sister. 

"Deoromakir" takes from "meorokir", "makir" and "deokir", which means "The daughter of one of my parent's sibling".
